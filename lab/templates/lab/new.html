    About Us

    This is the official website for the Physics Laboratory at KMITL. The Physics Laboratory was established in 2016 and serves as a platform providing facilities and administrative support for faculty research that transcends traditional departmental boundaries. Examples include collaborations between physics and engineering or physics and agricultural sciences. The concept of an independent laboratory is a distinctive feature of KMITL, fostering interdisciplinary collaboration and enabling world-class research and learning opportunities.

    Research

    Refer Link

    Date: 24th October, 2024

    Recent advancements in robotic arm technology have paved the way for unprecedented precision in manipulating microscopic structures. The Physics Laboratory collaborates with the Engineering Department to leverage robotic arms equipped with transistor-based sensors, enabling breakthroughs in nanotechnology and materials science. These tools are being used to analyze and innovate at the molecular level, setting new standards in laboratory research.

    Referral

    Date: 22nd January, 2024

    Our AI-integrated robotic systems are now being used to automate complex experiments that require high precision and minimal error margins. Through machine learning algorithms and innovative hardware, these robotic arms have significantly enhanced research capabilities. Projects include the study of microscopic material properties, real-time monitoring of quantum effects, and automation of repetitive tasks, allowing researchers to focus on critical analysis and interpretation.
